## Detailed Description of the Drawings

Referring to FIG. 1, the system architecture for a production-backed digital currency system is shown. The production-backed digital currency system (100) comprises four principal modules: a currency engine (102) configured to mint units of digital currency proportional to verified physical resource production across a plurality of resource categories (120) using configurable resource-to-currency weights, a production verification module (104) configured to validate production reports through bounds checking, statistical anomaly detection, cryptographic hash integrity verification, and multi-signature auditor approval, a monetary policy engine (106) configured to determine a policy phase based on current inflation relative to a configurable target and to adjust interest rates and currency supply using a Taylor-rule variant adapted for production-backed currency, and a federation governance module (108) configured to coordinate a plurality of autonomous economic zones (112) using trust-derived authority levels computed via exponential decay of operational divergence with exponential moving average smoothing. All currency transactions are recorded in an append-only hash-chain audit ledger (110). The autonomous economic zones (112) comprise zone A (114), zone B (116), and zone C (118), each operating as an independent economic entity within the federation.

Referring to FIG. 2, the currency minting and transaction lifecycle is shown. Currency minting (200) begins with a production report (202) specifying quantities of physical resources produced across a plurality of categories. Resource-to-currency weight calculation (204) computes the mint amount as a weighted sum of verified production quantities using configurable resource-to-currency weights. Multi-signature auditor approval (206) gates the minting process, requiring a configurable number of independent auditors to cryptographically sign the production report before currency minting is permitted. Minted currency enters the currency supply (208). Currency may be transferred between zones via inter-zone transfer (210) from a source zone (212) to a destination zone (214). Supply contraction (216) is achieved through burn operations (218) or reserve locking (220). The hash-chain audit ledger (222) records every transaction (224), computing an audit hash (226) from the previous transaction's hash concatenated with the current transaction details including verified production quantities, resource category weights, auditor signatures, and zone identifiers, creating a tamper-evident chain (228) that enables independent verification that each unit of currency is backed by a specific auditor-attested production event.

Referring to FIG. 3, the production verification pipeline is shown. A production report submission (300) passes through four sequential verification stages. Bounds checking (302) rejects reports containing negative quantities (304). Statistical anomaly detection (306) computes anomaly scores for each production quantity against the zone's historical production distribution and flags quantities exceeding a configurable anomaly threshold for manual review (308). Cryptographic hash integrity verification (310) rejects tampered reports (312). Multi-signature auditor approval (314) requires sufficient independent auditor signatures before approving; insufficient signatures result in pending approval status (316). Reports that pass all four stages are classified as verified production (318) and become eligible for currency minting (320).

Referring to FIG. 4, the monetary policy engine state diagram is shown. The monetary policy engine operates in four phases: neutral (400) when inflation is within a configurable target band, expansionary (402) when inflation is below a configurable lower bound, contractionary (404) when inflation is above a configurable upper bound, and emergency (406) when inflation exceeds a configurable emergency threshold. The engine transitions between phases based on the relationship of current inflation to the configurable thresholds. The Taylor-rule variant (408) computes the interest rate as a function of the equilibrium interest rate, the deviation of current inflation from the target, and the deviation of current growth from a target growth rate, with configurable coefficients for each deviation term. Interest rate adjustment (410) and currency supply adjustment (412) implement the policy decisions. Reserve ratio management (414) maintains the reserve ratio within configurable bounds and prioritizes reserve replenishment when the ratio falls below a configurable minimum.

Referring to FIG. 5, the federation governance pipeline is shown. Zone instances (500) are governed through a multi-stage governance pipeline. The observe stage (502) monitors operational metrics including response rate (514), average latency (516), and partition detection indicators (518), maintaining a sliding window of interaction outcomes for federation health assessment. The assess trust stage (504) computes trust via exponential decay of operational divergence (522) with EMA smoothing (524) and recovery boost (526) that temporarily increases the smoothing responsiveness during the recovering phase to allow faster trust recovery after partition healing. The check freeze conditions stage (506) detects network partitions based on instance response rates and freezes federation operations when fewer than a configurable fraction of instances are responding, preventing split-brain currency issuance. The modulate authority stage (508) maps trust levels to trust-derived authority levels (528), determining whether zones operate with autonomous minting authority (530) at high trust, co-signature requirements (532) at medium trust, or full consensus requirements (534) at low trust. The enforce phases stage (510) classifies federation state into synchronized (538), diverging (540), partitioned (542), and recovering (544) phases.

Referring to FIG. 6, the universal basic income distribution process is shown. A periodic distribution trigger (600) initiates the distribution cycle. The current monetary policy phase is checked (602) to determine the distribution amount: expanded amount (604) during the expansionary phase, standard amount (606) during the neutral phase, reduced amount (608) during the contractionary phase, or suspended distribution (610) during the emergency phase. A zone eligibility check (612) determines whether each zone qualifies for distribution. Eligible zones receive currency distribution (614), while ineligible zones are skipped (616). Each distribution is recorded in an audit record (618).

Referring to FIG. 7, the inter-zone settlement process is shown. Zone A (700) initiates a trade with the settlement engine (702), providing a reference identifier linking the currency transfer to a physical resource trade. The settlement engine validates the balance (708) of the initiating zone and records trade details (710). The settlement engine debits the amount from zone A (712) and credits the corresponding amount to zone B (714). The settlement engine records the physical resource trade reference link (716) in the hash-chain audit ledger (706), associating the currency transfer with the underlying physical resource trade via the reference identifier. The audit hash chain (718) is extended to maintain the tamper-evident record of the settlement.
