## Closest Prior Art (ranked by relevance)

### 1. Algorithmic Stablecoins with Taylor Rule Adaptation
- **Source:** arXiv:1909.07445 ("Truthful and Faithful Monetary Policy for a Stablecoin"); arXiv:2104.07888 ("Optimal Algorithmic Monetary Policy")
- **Date:** 2019-2021
- **What they cover:**
  - Algorithmic monetary policy for stablecoins using rule-based supply adjustment
  - Taylor rule framework adapted for cryptocurrency supply management
  - Model Predictive Control for supply adjustments based on price deviations
  - Decentralized, algorithmic monetary policy removing human intervention
- **What they DON'T cover:**
  - No production-backed currency — these are fiat-pegged or algorithmic stablecoins
  - No physical resource verification (bounds checking, statistical anomaly detection, multi-signature auditor approval)
  - No federation governance with trust-derived authority across economic zones
  - No resource-to-currency weight mapping across multiple resource categories
  - No partition detection or split-brain prevention
  - No UBI distribution module
  - Taylor rule applied to price stability, not production-backed inflation targeting
- **Claims affected:** Claims 1(c), 2(g-h), 7 (Taylor rule concept)
- **Relevance:** HIGH — Directly addresses algorithmic monetary policy with Taylor rule variants for digital currency. Key differentiator: These target price-pegged stability; Terra Unita's Taylor rule variant targets production-backed inflation with resource-category weights, growth deviations, and federation governance. The backing mechanism is fundamentally different (no backing vs. verified physical production).

## Recommendation

### Strong Claims (proceed as-is)
- **Claims 4, 9, 10, 11, 17**: Highly novel. Multi-category production-backed minting, the federation governance pipeline, partition detection for split-brain prevention, federation state classification, and recovery boost have no prior art.

### Claims Needing Minor Narrowing
- **Claims 1, 2, 3 (independent)**: Should emphasize the integration of:
  1. Currency minting proportional to **verified physical resource production** across multiple categories (distinguishes from commodity-backed tokens which require deposit, not production)
  2. **Production verification** with bounds checking + statistical anomaly detection + multi-signature auditor approval (distinguishes from simple reserve attestation)
  3. **Taylor rule variant adapted for production-backed currency** with growth deviation (distinguishes from price-peg Taylor rule adaptations)
  4. **Federation governance** with trust-derived authority from exponential decay of operational divergence (distinguishes from standard blockchain governance)

- **Claim 7**: Taylor rule variants for crypto exist in academic literature. Ensure the claim emphasizes the production-backed adaptation (growth deviation from production targets, not price deviation from peg).

- **Claim 13**: Blockchain UBI exists (Circles, GoodDollar). The novel element is scaling UBI by monetary policy phase — ensure this is the focus.

- **Claim 20**: Supply adjustment mechanisms exist in algorithmic stablecoins. Narrow to emphasize the production-backed context and dual mechanism (mint/release vs. burn/lock).

### At-Risk Claims
- **Claim 12** (hash-chain audit ledger): Standard blockchain construction. Weak standalone novelty. Keep as dependent claim describing the specific audit structure.

### Overall Assessment
**STRONG NOVELTY.** The core concept of minting currency proportional to verified physical resource production (energy, food, water, materials) with algorithmic monetary policy is fundamentally different from all found prior art:
- Commodity-backed tokens are storage receipts, not production rewards
- Algorithmic stablecoins adjust supply based on price, not production verification
- Blockchain UBI distributes based on time or DeFi yields, not production
- Taylor rule adaptations target price stability, not production-backed inflation

The federation governance with trust-derived authority, partition detection preventing split-brain currency issuance, and multi-stage governance pipeline are all novel with no close prior art. The weakest element is the hash-chain audit ledger (Claim 12), which is standard blockchain technology.
