**Title:** Systems and Methods for Production-Backed Digital Currency with Algorithmic Monetary Policy and Federated Governance

**Problem:** Existing digital currencies are disconnected from productive economic activity. Fiat-backed stablecoins inherit inflationary and political risks. Cryptocurrencies issue currency for computation or staked capital, not productive output. No system issues a general-purpose currency backed by verified production across multiple resource categories with algorithmic monetary policy targeting production-relative inflation and federated governance coordinating autonomous economic zones.

**Solution:** A production-backed digital currency system comprising: (1) a currency engine that mints digital currency proportional to verified physical production across configurable resource categories (energy, food, water, materials) with multi-signature auditor verification; (2) a multi-stage production verification pipeline using bounds checking, statistical anomaly detection, hash integrity, and multi-auditor approval; (3) an algorithmic monetary policy engine using a Taylor-rule variant adapted for production-backed currency, operating in four phases with reserve management; and (4) a federation governance framework using exponential decay trust with EMA smoothing, partition detection, and trust-derived authority levels.

**Key Innovation:** Multi-resource production-backed currency issuance with integrated algorithmic monetary policy. Currency value is anchored to verified physical production across multiple resource categories rather than to fiat reserves, computational work, or staked tokens. The monetary policy engine targets inflation relative to production growth (not a fiat peg), and the federated governance enables autonomous economic zones to operate independently while preventing split-brain currency issuance during network partitions.

**Claims Summary:** 3 independent claims (system, method, computer-readable medium) covering production-backed minting, multi-stage verification, algorithmic monetary policy, and federation governance. 17 dependent claims covering Taylor-rule computation, money velocity, policy phases, reserve management, UBI distribution, settlement engine, partition detection, and recovery mechanisms.

**Prior Art Differentiation:**
- Fiat-backed stablecoins (USDT, USDC): Backed by fiat reserves, not physical production; centralized issuance; no production verification
- Bitcoin/Ethereum: Currency issued for computation or stake, not productive output; no monetary policy engine
- Carbon credits / RECs: Single resource type, not multi-resource general-purpose currency; no algorithmic monetary policy; no federation governance
- Central bank digital currencies (CBDCs): Government-issued, centralized; no multi-resource backing; no decentralized federation
- Algorithmic stablecoins (Terra/Luna): Target fiat peg, not production-relative inflation; no production verification; no federated governance
