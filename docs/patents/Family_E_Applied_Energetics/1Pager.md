**Title:** Systems and Methods for Cross-Domain Resonance Analysis and Anti-Fragile Resource Allocation Using Coupled Oscillator Models

**Problem:** Complex intelligent systems span multiple heterogeneous domains (cognitive, financial, memory, analytical), each with different temporal dynamics and oscillatory patterns. No unified metric exists to quantify cross-domain coherence. Conventional risk management uniformly reduces exposure during uncertainty, but certain instrument classes (convex payoff profiles) actually benefit from volatility. No framework exists for systematically increasing exposure to uncertainty-benefiting instruments while maintaining governance on standard instruments.

**Solution:** A cross-domain resonance analysis framework with three components: (1) an E-R-I (Energy-Resonance-Information) analytical model that quantifies coupling between heterogeneous domains along three orthogonal axes; (2) a coupled oscillator model where trust modulates coupling strength and a synchronization order parameter provides a physics-based coherence metric converted to governance-compatible trust signals; and (3) an anti-fragile resource allocation engine that selectively increases exposure for convex-payoff instruments during high uncertainty while maintaining standard governance for linear instruments.

**Key Innovation:** Trust-modulated coupled oscillator dynamics applied to heterogeneous computing subsystems. By representing each domain as an oscillator with natural frequency proportional to its update rate and coupling strength modulated by mutual trust, the system creates a self-reinforcing feedback loop: synchronization builds trust, which strengthens coupling, which promotes further synchronization. The resulting order parameter is a single scalar that captures system-wide coherence across domains operating at vastly different temporal scales, and the anti-fragile allocation engine inverts the traditional response to uncertainty for qualifying instruments.

**Claims Summary:** 3 independent claims (system, method, computer-readable medium) covering coupled oscillator model with trust-modulated coupling, order parameter computation, trust conversion, and anti-fragile resource allocation. 15 dependent claims covering E-R-I framework, dual-governance integration, signal confidence modifiers, fallback computation, and resonance reporting.

**Prior Art Differentiation:**
- Kuramoto model (physics): Known for biological oscillators, never applied to heterogeneous computing governance with trust-modulated coupling
- Portfolio risk management (Markowitz, VaR): Uniformly reduces exposure during uncertainty; no anti-fragile inversion for convex instruments
- Multi-agent coordination: Communication-based, not physics-based synchronization
- Transfer entropy / Granger causality: Domain-specific, require common sampling rates; E-R-I framework is domain-agnostic
- Taleb anti-fragility (concept): Philosophical framework, no computational implementation with governance gating
