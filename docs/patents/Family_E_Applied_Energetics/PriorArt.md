### 1. Kuramoto Model — Mathematical Foundation
- **Source:** Wikipedia; Acebrón et al., Rev. Mod. Phys. 77, 137 (2005); various academic papers
- **Date:** 1975 (original), ongoing research
- **What it covers:** The Kuramoto model describes synchronization of coupled oscillators with natural frequencies and coupling strength. Computes an order parameter (r) measuring phase coherence. Well-established in physics, neuroscience, power grid analysis, and social systems.
- **What it DOESN'T cover:** No application to trust/governance systems; no trust-modulated coupling; no conversion of order parameter to trust-compatible signals; no E-R-I (Energy-Resonance-Information) framework; no anti-fragile resource allocation; no connection to governance phases; no exponential decay trust conversion.
- **Claims affected:** Claims 1(a), 1(c) (mathematical foundation only)
- **Relevance:** HIGH for mathematical basis, LOW for application domain — The Kuramoto model is the foundation but the application to governance/trust and the E-R-I framework are entirely novel.

## Recommendation

### Strong Claims (proceed as-is)
- **Claims 4, 8, 9, 10, 15, 16, 18**: Highly novel. The E-R-I framework is entirely new with no prior art. Anti-fragile allocation gated by dual-governance with specific boost formulas has no precedent.

### Claims Needing Minor Narrowing
- **Claims 1, 2, 3**: Independent claims should emphasize the specific novel combination: (a) trust-modulated coupling (not just Kuramoto), (b) order parameter to trust-compatible signal conversion via exponential decay, and (c) anti-fragile resource allocation based on payoff profile classification. The Kuramoto model itself is well-established prior art, so claims should focus on the novel application domain and trust/governance integration.
- **Claim 5**: Standard Kuramoto coupling formula — ensure claim language emphasizes the trust-modulation aspect, not just the coupling mechanism.

### At-Risk Claims
- **Claim 14**: Fallback computation modes are common engineering practice. This claim is weak as a standalone patent claim. Consider making it a dependent claim focused on the specific oscillator math being preserved in fallback.

### Overall Assessment
**STRONG NOVELTY.** The Kuramoto model is well-known in physics, but its application to governance trust systems with trust-modulated coupling, E-R-I framework, and anti-fragile resource allocation is entirely novel. No prior art was found that combines coupled oscillator models with governance coordination, trust-based coupling strength, or anti-fragile resource allocation based on payoff profile classification. The E-R-I (Energy-Resonance-Information) three-axis framework appears to be completely original with zero prior art.
