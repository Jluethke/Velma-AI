# Example: Building a Daily Meditation Habit

## Input
```
target_habit: "Meditate for 15 minutes daily"
current_habits: ["Wake up at 7am", "Make coffee", "Check phone", "Shower", "Commute"]
goals: ["Reduce anxiety", "Improve focus at work", "Be more present with family"]
available_time: { "morning": "7:00-8:00", "evening": "21:00-22:00" }
barriers: ["Tried headspace for 2 weeks last year, stopped when I missed a weekend", "Feel restless sitting still", "Morning feels rushed"]
```

## OBSERVE Output
**Habit classification:** Daily, time-bound (15 min target), mental/contemplative.
**Stacking anchors:** "Make coffee" is strongest -- consistent, daily, has a natural wait period while coffee brews.
**Two-minute version:** Sit in a chair, close eyes, take 5 deep breaths. (~90 seconds)
**Barrier analysis:**
- Previous failure: weekend disruption broke streak -> all-or-nothing thinking killed the habit
- Restlessness: starting at 15 minutes is too long for a beginner
- Morning rush: perception of no time, but 2 minutes exists in any morning
**Identity statement:** "I am someone who starts each day grounded and calm."

## REASON Output
**Implementation intention:** "After I press start on the coffee maker, I will sit in the kitchen chair, close my eyes, and breathe for [current week's duration]. My reward is my first sip of coffee."

**Environment modifications:**
- Move a comfortable kitchen chair to face the window (not the phone charging station)
- Phone stays plugged in across the room until after meditation
- Place a small card on the coffee maker: "Coffee is brewing. Sit."
- Download a timer app with a gentle bell (not Headspace -- remove the app dependency)

**Bright line rule:** "Did I sit and close my eyes after pressing the coffee maker? Yes/No."

## PLAN Output: 30-Day Progression

### Week 1: Anchor Phase (Days 1-7)
- **Daily minimum:** 2 minutes (5 deep breaths + sit quietly)
- **Goal:** Do it every single day. Quality doesn't matter. Just sit.
- **Milestone:** Day 3 -- you've done it more times than most attempts last. Day 7 -- first full week.

### Week 2: Consistency Phase (Days 8-14)
- **Daily minimum:** 5 minutes (set timer, focus on breath)
- **Goal:** Start using the timer. Notice when your mind wanders -- that's the practice.
- **Milestone:** Day 14 -- two full weeks. You are now ahead of your last attempt.
- **Note:** If you missed any days in week 1, stay at 2 minutes for days 8-10, then increase.

### Week 3: Growth Phase (Days 15-21)
- **Daily minimum:** 10 minutes
- **Goal:** Developing comfort with stillness. Begin noticing the effect on your morning.
- **Weekend adjustment:** Weekends are the previous failure point. Set a phone alarm for "coffee + sit" even if wake time varies.
- **Milestone:** Day 21 -- neurological habit formation threshold. This should feel semi-automatic now.

### Week 4: Identity Phase (Days 22-30)
- **Daily minimum:** 15 minutes (full target)
- **Goal:** This is who you are now. "I meditate" not "I'm trying to meditate."
- **Milestone:** Day 30 -- one month. Evaluate: continue at 15 min, or explore longer sits?

### Miss Protocol
If you miss a day: the next morning, do the 2-minute version. Non-negotiable. Don't try to "make up" the missed session. Just show up for 2 minutes and get back on track.
