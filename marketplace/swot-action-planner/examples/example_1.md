# SWOT Action Planner -- EdTech Startup

## Scenario

A Series A EdTech startup (online coding bootcamp, $4M ARR, 50 employees) is doing annual strategic planning. The CEO has rough SWOT inputs from a leadership offsite and needs them transformed into a prioritized, trackable action plan for Q2.

## Input SWOT

```
STRENGTHS:
S1. 92% job placement rate (industry-leading)
S2. Strong instructor team (ex-FAANG engineers)
S3. Proprietary project-based curriculum
S4. High NPS (72) from graduates

WEAKNESSES:
W1. No brand recognition outside tech Twitter
W2. High CAC ($3,200 per enrolled student)
W3. No enterprise/B2B sales motion
W4. Manual grading bottleneck (limits scale)

OPPORTUNITIES:
O1. Corporate upskilling market growing 25% YoY
O2. AI coding tools create demand for "AI-augmented developer" training
O3. International markets untapped (LATAM, India)
O4. Government workforce development grants available

THREATS:
T1. Free coding resources (freeCodeCamp, YouTube) reducing WTP
T2. Economic downturn reducing discretionary education spending
T3. Big players entering (Google Career Certificates, Coursera)
T4. AI might reduce demand for junior developers (perception risk)
```

## Execution: ORPA Loop

### OBSERVE

**SWOT quality check:**
- Strengths: 4 items, all specific and measurable. Good quality.
- Weaknesses: 4 items, clear and actionable. Good quality.
- Opportunities: 4 items, externally-focused, time-relevant. Good quality.
- Threats: 4 items, specific and current. Good quality.

**Balance assessment:**
- Internal (S+W): 8 items, balanced
- External (O+T): 8 items, balanced
- Positive (S+O): 8 items
- Negative (W+T): 8 items
- Assessment: Well-balanced SWOT, no category is thin

**Missing considerations:**
- No regulatory/compliance items (SWOT gap)
- No mention of financial position (runway, profitability)
- Technology stack strengths/weaknesses not captured

### REASON

**TOWS Matrix -- Cross-Reference Analysis:**

**SO Strategies (Use Strengths to Capture Opportunities):**
```
SO1: S1+S2 x O1 = Launch enterprise upskilling program
  Use the 92% placement rate and ex-FAANG instructors as proof points
  to sell corporate training contracts. B2B deal sizes are 10-50x
  consumer, and instructor credibility matters more in enterprise.

SO2: S3 x O2 = Build "AI-Augmented Developer" bootcamp
  Leverage proprietary curriculum to be first with an AI-focused
  developer program. S3 (project-based) makes this credible.
  First-mover advantage in a growing niche.

SO3: S4 x O4 = Apply for government workforce grants
  92% placement + 72 NPS = strong grant application. Government
  wants outcomes data. We have the best in the industry.
```

**WO Strategies (Fix Weaknesses to Capture Opportunities):**
```
WO1: W1 x O1 = Partner with enterprises for co-branded programs
  Fixing brand recognition through enterprise partnerships.
  "Powered by [us]" inside corporate learning portals.
  Enterprise brand lends credibility we lack.

WO2: W2 x O3 = International expansion with lower CAC markets
  CAC is $3,200 in the US. In LATAM/India, digital acquisition
  costs are 40-60% lower. Same curriculum, lower CAC.

WO3: W4 x O2 = AI-powered automated grading
  Build AI grading to remove manual bottleneck. Positions us
  as AI-native (aligned with O2) while solving scale limitation.
```

**ST Strategies (Use Strengths to Counter Threats):**
```
ST1: S1 x T1 = Double down on outcomes marketing
  Free resources can teach coding. They can't guarantee jobs.
  Amplify the 92% placement rate -- this is the moat against free.
  "Free teaches you to code. We get you hired."

ST2: S2+S3 x T3 = Differentiate on instructor quality + live projects
  Google and Coursera offer recorded content at scale. We offer
  live instruction from engineers who built systems at scale.
  This is a feature they can't replicate at their price point.

ST3: S3 x T4 = Reposition: "We train developers who work WITH AI"
  Address the perception that AI replaces developers by positioning
  graduates as AI-augmented (more valuable, not less).
```

**WT Strategies (Minimize Weaknesses Against Threats):**
```
WT1: W1 x T3 = Niche positioning (don't compete on brand with Google)
  We can't out-brand Google. Instead, own a specific niche:
  "The bootcamp for career changers who want FAANG-level jobs."
  Niche = defensible against generalist competitors.

WT2: W2 x T2 = Income Share Agreements to reduce price sensitivity
  High CAC + economic downturn = bad combo. ISAs reduce upfront
  cost barrier. Student pays after getting a job. De-risks for
  the student, aligns incentives with our 92% placement rate.

WT3: W3 x T2 = Enterprise contracts as recession hedge
  B2C education spending drops in downturns. B2B upskilling budgets
  are more resilient ("reskill existing employees vs hire new").
  Building B2B revenue diversifies away from consumer risk.
```

### ACT

**Prioritized Action Plan:**

```
ACTION 1: Launch Enterprise Upskilling Pilot
  Owner: VP Sales (hire if needed)
  Timeline: Q2 (April-June)
  Steps:
    Week 1-2: Define enterprise offering (cohort-based, 10-20 employees)
    Week 3-4: Build sales deck leveraging 92% placement + FAANG instructors
    Week 5-8: Outbound to 50 mid-market companies (L&D / HR buyer)
    Week 9-12: Close 2-3 pilot contracts ($50K-$150K each)
  Success Metrics:
    - 3 pilot contracts signed by end of Q2
    - Pipeline of $500K+ in enterprise opportunities
    - Pilot NPS > 60
  Resources: 1 enterprise AE, sales deck, pilot curriculum adaptation
  Budget: $40K (AE comp for 3 months + materials)

ACTION 2: Outcomes Marketing Campaign
  Owner: Head of Marketing
  Timeline: April (launch within 2 weeks)
  Steps:
    Week 1: Produce 5 graduate success stories (video + written)
    Week 2: Launch "92% placement rate" campaign (paid social + SEO)
    Week 3-4: Place stories in tech publications (TechCrunch, Hacker News)
    Ongoing: A/B test messaging: outcomes vs curriculum vs instructors
  Success Metrics:
    - 30% increase in organic traffic from branded search
    - 20% reduction in CAC from improved conversion
    - 5 press mentions featuring placement rate
  Resources: marketing team + $15K ad spend
  Budget: $20K total

ACTION 3: AI-Augmented Developer Curriculum
  Owner: Head of Curriculum
  Timeline: Q2 design, Q3 launch
  Steps:
    Week 1-4: Research AI coding tools (Copilot, Cursor, etc.)
    Week 5-8: Design 4 AI-integrated project modules
    Week 9-12: Beta test with current cohort, gather feedback
  Success Metrics:
    - Curriculum designed and beta-tested by end of Q2
    - 80% of beta students rate AI modules as "valuable"
    - Ready for marketing launch in Q3
  Resources: 2 curriculum designers, 1 AI engineer
  Budget: $25K (contractor + tool licenses)

ACTION 4: Reposition Messaging Around AI
  Owner: CEO + Head of Marketing
  Timeline: April (immediate)
  Steps:
    Week 1: Craft messaging: "We train developers who build WITH AI"
    Week 2: Update website, social profiles, sales materials
    Week 3: CEO thought leadership post on LinkedIn/Medium
    Week 4: Update all ad copy and landing pages
  Success Metrics:
    - Website messaging updated by April 15
    - CEO post gets 10K+ impressions
    - Enrollment inquiry mentions of "AI concern" decrease by 50%
  Resources: marketing team, CEO time
  Budget: $2K (design updates)

ACTION 5: AI Grading Prototype
  Owner: CTO
  Timeline: Q2 prototype, Q3 deployment
  Steps:
    Week 1-4: Evaluate AI grading approaches (LLM-based code review)
    Week 5-8: Build prototype for 2 project types
    Week 9-12: A/B test AI grading vs manual on accuracy and speed
  Success Metrics:
    - Prototype grades with 85% agreement vs human graders
    - Reduces grading time by 60%
    - Ready for production testing in Q3
  Resources: 1 ML engineer, 1 backend engineer
  Budget: $15K (API costs + engineering time)

TOTAL Q2 INVESTMENT: $102K
EXPECTED Q2 IMPACT: Foundation for $500K+ enterprise pipeline,
  20% CAC reduction, AI curriculum ready for Q3 launch
```

**SWOT review cadence:** Re-run SWOT at end of Q2. Check:
- Did any strengths erode?
- Did any weaknesses get addressed?
- Are opportunities still open?
- Did any new threats emerge?
- Update action plan for Q3 based on revised SWOT.
