# Market Entry Analyzer -- HR Tech for Mid-Market

## Scenario

A B2B SaaS company (currently serving SMBs with a general project management tool) wants to enter the mid-market HR tech space with an employee engagement platform. The VP of Strategy needs a structured analysis before the board commits $2M to the initiative.

## Execution: Phase Pipeline

### Phase 1: MARKET -- Size and Dynamics

**Market sizing:**

```
TAM (Total Addressable Market):
  Method: Top-down
  Global HR tech market: $35B (2026)
  Employee engagement segment: ~12% = $4.2B
  Growth rate: 14% CAGR
  TAM = $4.2B

SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market):
  Geography: US only (initial)
  US share of global HR tech: ~40% = $1.68B
  Mid-market only (100-1000 employees): ~35% = $588M
  SAM = $588M

SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):
  Realistic first 3-year capture: 0.5-1% of SAM
  SOM = $2.9M - $5.9M (Year 3 target ARR)

Bottom-up validation:
  US mid-market companies (100-1000 employees): ~200,000
  % likely to buy engagement tool: 30% = 60,000 addressable
  Average ACV for mid-market: $15,000
  If we capture 200 customers by Year 3: $3M ARR
  Consistent with top-down SOM.
```

**Market dynamics:**
```
Growth drivers:
  - Remote/hybrid work increasing demand for engagement tools
  - HR compliance complexity increasing
  - Labor market tight -- retention is priority #1 for HR
  - AI-powered people analytics emerging as differentiator

Headwinds:
  - Economic uncertainty reducing software budgets
  - Consolidation: large HRIS platforms adding engagement features
  - SMB tools moving upmarket (competition from below)
  - Buyer fatigue: HR teams already have too many tools
```

### Phase 3: POSITION -- Differentiation

**Competitive gaps identified:**
```
1. ACTION GAP: All competitors measure engagement. None close the loop
   with action planning and tracking. Surveys without action = survey fatigue.

2. PRICE GAP: Mid-market is underserved. Enterprise tools are too expensive
   ($8-15/emp/mo). SMB tools lack mid-market features (SSO, HRIS integration,
   compliance). Sweet spot: $4-6/emp/mo with mid-market features.

3. SPEED GAP: Average implementation is 6-8 weeks. A 1-week deployment
   would differentiate significantly.

4. AI GAP: Most competitors use basic analytics. AI-driven action
   recommendations (not just insights) are emerging but not yet standard.
```

**Positioning statement:**
```
"For mid-market HR leaders who need to improve employee engagement
but are frustrated by expensive, slow-to-implement survey tools that
generate data without driving action -- [Product] is an employee
engagement platform that turns engagement signals into concrete
manager actions within 1 week of deployment, at half the price of
enterprise alternatives."
```

### Phase 4: STRATEGY -- Go-to-Market Plan

```
CHANNEL STRATEGY:
  Primary:  Content marketing + SEO (HR leaders Google problems)
  Secondary: HR conference sponsorships (SHRM, HR Tech)
  Tertiary:  HRIS integration marketplace (BambooHR, Rippling)
  Sales:     Inside sales team (2 AEs + 1 SDR initially)

MESSAGING:
  Headline: "Engagement insights that drive action, not just reports"
  Proof points: 1-week deployment, action planning built-in, $4/emp/mo
  Objection handling:
    "We already have a tool" -> "Does it close the loop with actions?"
    "We're too small for this" -> "Built for 100-1000 employees"
    "What about our HRIS?" -> "Integrates with [top 5] in 24 hours"

PRICING:
  Starter:    $3/emp/mo (surveys + basic analytics, <200 employees)
  Pro:        $5/emp/mo (action planning, manager tools, HRIS integration)
  Enterprise: $8/emp/mo (custom analytics, API, dedicated CSM, SSO)

LAUNCH TIMELINE:
  Month 1-2:   Beta with 10 design partners (free, feedback in exchange)
  Month 3:     Public launch, content marketing begins
  Month 4-6:   First 20 paying customers, refine ICP
  Month 7-12:  Scale to 50 customers, hire 2 more AEs
  Year 2:      Target 100 customers, $1.5M ARR

PARTNERSHIPS:
  - HRIS integrations: BambooHR, Rippling, Gusto (marketplace listings)
  - HR consultancies: referral partnerships with implementation firms
  - Industry analysts: Gartner, Forrester briefings for inclusion in reports
```

### Phase 5: RISKS -- Assessment and Kill Criteria

```
RISK MATRIX:
Risk                    Probability  Impact   Mitigation
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Incumbents add action     High       High     Move fast, build moat with
features                                       AI recommendations + data
                          
Slow mid-market sales     Medium     High     Start with SMBs moving up,
cycle (3-6 months)                             offer free pilot period

HRIS platforms bundle     Medium     Medium   Position as best-of-breed,
engagement natively                            emphasize integration depth

Can't hire HR domain      Medium     Medium   Hire 1 senior HR product
expertise                                      person as first hire

Engagement fatigue        Low        High     Differentiate on action,
(market saturated)                             not just measurement

KILL CRITERIA (review at Month 9):
  KILL if:
  - <5 paying customers after 6 months of selling
  - CAC > $25,000 (unsustainable unit economics)
  - Win rate < 10% in competitive deals
  - NPS from beta users < 30
  - Total investment exceeds $1M with <$100K ARR

  PIVOT if:
  - SMBs convert faster than mid-market -> pivot downmarket
  - One vertical dominates (e.g., healthcare) -> go vertical-first
  - Engagement is wrong entry point -> pivot to performance or L&D

  DOUBLE DOWN if:
  - >20 paying customers in 6 months
  - Win rate > 25% in competitive deals
  - NPS > 50 from early customers
  - Expansion revenue appearing (upsells within accounts)
```
